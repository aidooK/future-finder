## About This Opportunity

The French Government Excellence Scholarship is managed by Campus France and made available to outstanding international students — including those from African countries — pursuing Master's or PhD degrees at French higher education institutions. Unlike the Eiffel Scholarship (which is employer-nominated), this programme operates through bilateral agreements between France and partner countries, meaning eligibility, funding levels, and deadlines vary significantly depending on your country of origin.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Citizen of a country with an active bilateral scholarship agreement with France (most African countries qualify — confirm through your local Campus France office or French embassy)
- Meet the academic entry requirements for your chosen French institution and programme
- Applications are generally submitted through the French embassy or Campus France office in your home country, not directly to French universities
- Demonstrate strong academic achievement and a clear study plan in France

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend to support living costs during your studies in France (amount varies by country and level)
- Tuition fee support or exemption, depending on your country's bilateral arrangement
- Access to Campus France's support services for international students in France

## How to Apply

1. Visit [campusfrance.org/en](https://www.campusfrance.org/en) and search for your country's specific page to confirm availability and requirements
2. Contact the Campus France office or French embassy in Ghana to confirm the current scholarship round details, amounts, and application steps
3. Prepare your academic transcripts, motivation letter, and any documents required by your specific bilateral arrangement
4. Submit your application through the designated channel in Ghana (typically the French embassy or Campus France Ghana)
5. Deadlines and notification timelines vary by country — confirm early

## Tips

- This scholarship is less uniform than the Eiffel programme — the amount, process, and availability are all determined by Ghana's specific bilateral arrangement with France. Contact the French Embassy in Accra or Campus France Ghana first to understand exactly what is on offer for Ghanaian applicants.
- If you are targeting France for postgraduate study, apply for both this scholarship and the Eiffel Excellence Scholarship simultaneously if eligible — the two programmes have different tracks and do not always overlap.
- French language proficiency is a strong asset even if your target programme is taught in English, as daily life in France operates in French.

**⏰ Deadline varies by country — contact Campus France Ghana or the French Embassy in Accra for the current Ghanaian deadline and application process.**
