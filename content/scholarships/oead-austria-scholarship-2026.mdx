## About This Opportunity

OeAD (Austria's Agency for Education and Internationalisation) administers a range of scholarship programmes on behalf of the Austrian government, supporting international students to pursue postgraduate studies and research at Austrian universities and institutions. The programmes cover Master's, PhD, and research levels across various fields, with funding available to applicants from a wide range of countries including those in Africa.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Eligibility varies by specific OeAD programme — confirm which programme applies to Ghanaian nationals on oead.at
- Generally requires a relevant first or postgraduate degree with strong academic standing
- Applications go through either OeAD directly or through Austria's bilateral channels depending on your country and the specific programme
- German language proficiency may be required for some programmes; English-taught options are available for others

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend to cover living costs during your studies in Austria
- Accommodation support and contribution toward housing costs
- Travel allowance to and from Austria for qualifying programmes
- Health insurance coverage for the duration of the scholarship

## How to Apply

1. Visit [oead.at/en](https://oead.at/en/) and browse the full list of scholarship programmes by country and academic level
2. Identify the specific programme(s) relevant to Ghanaian nationals at your academic level
3. Contact the Austrian Embassy in Ghana or OeAD directly to confirm the application process for your target programme
4. Prepare your application documents — transcripts, research proposal or motivation letter, references, and language certificates
5. Submit before the programme-specific deadline (which varies between February and September depending on the award)

## Tips

- OeAD manages multiple distinct scholarship programmes, each with its own eligibility rules and deadlines — do not apply generically. Identify the specific award you qualify for first.
- Austrian universities have a strong reputation in music, fine arts, sciences, and engineering — if your field aligns with these, Austria is a genuinely compelling academic destination.
- German language courses are widely available in Austria even after arrival, but having some basic proficiency before you go will significantly ease your transition.

**⏰ Deadlines vary by programme, falling between February and September (Annual) — visit oead.at and confirm your specific programme's deadline and eligibility.**
