## About This Opportunity

The DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) Research Grants support doctoral students and postdoctoral researchers from around the world to conduct extended research stays at German universities and research institutions. The programme is designed for academics who want to collaborate with German researchers, access Germany's world-class research infrastructure, or complete a portion of their doctoral or postdoctoral work in Germany. It is one of the most accessible pathways for African researchers to spend funded time in Germany.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Must be enrolled in a doctoral programme or hold a doctoral degree (for postdoctoral applicants)
- Strong academic and research track record relevant to your proposed research area
- Identify a German host supervisor or institution willing to support your stay
- Applications typically submitted through DAAD's online portal with supporting documentation

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend of €1,350 for doctoral candidates or €1,500 for postdoctoral researchers
- Travel allowance to and from Germany
- Health, accident, and liability insurance coverage during your stay
- Access to Germany's leading research institutions and academic networks

## How to Apply

1. Visit [daad.de](https://www2.daad.de) and search for Research Grants relevant to your academic level and field
2. Identify and contact a German host supervisor or research institution to confirm their willingness to host you
3. Prepare a detailed research proposal and plan for your stay in Germany
4. Complete and submit the DAAD online application, including transcripts, language certificates, and a letter of invitation from your host
5. Submit before the annual October deadline for the following academic year

## Tips

- Securing a German host supervisor's agreement before applying is effectively mandatory — without it, your application lacks a critical component.
- Your research proposal should be specific and technically convincing. DAAD reviewers assess academic merit and the feasibility of your proposed research agenda in Germany.
- German language skills are not always required but are a strong advantage — even basic proficiency signals commitment to integration in the German research community.

**⏰ Deadline: Typically October (Annual) — check daad.de for the exact current deadline and start connecting with potential German host supervisors at least 6 months in advance.**
