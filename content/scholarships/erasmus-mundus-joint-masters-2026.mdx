## About This Opportunity

Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's (EMJM) degrees are prestigious, integrated study programmes delivered jointly by a consortium of universities across at least two European countries. Funded by the European Union, this scholarship covers high-achieving students worldwide, including a strong allocation of places for applicants from Africa. Scholars study at multiple universities over the course of the programme, gaining an internationally recognised joint or multiple degree along with rich cross-cultural academic experience.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Hold a first degree (Bachelor's) relevant to your chosen Erasmus Mundus Master's programme
- Open to applicants of any nationality, with funding specifically allocated for non-EU applicants including Africans
- Must meet the specific academic and language requirements of your chosen joint programme (each programme sets its own criteria)
- Willingness and ability to study and relocate across at least two different European countries during the programme

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full tuition fees covered across the entire joint Master's programme
- Monthly subsistence stipend to cover living costs (varies by programme, typically around €1,000+/month)
- Travel allowance to and between host countries
- Installation costs to help you settle in your host countries
- An internationally recognised joint, double, or multiple degree upon graduation

## How to Apply

1. Visit [erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/opportunities/opportunities-for-individuals/students/erasmus-mundus-joint-masters) and browse the full catalogue of EMJM programmes by field of study
2. Select one or more joint Master's programmes that align with your academic background and interests
3. Apply directly to your chosen programme(s) through their individual consortium application portals — each has its own process and specific deadline within the broader window
4. Indicate your interest in being considered for the EU scholarship within the programme application
5. Submit all required documents, including transcripts, references, language certificates, and a statement of purpose

## Tips

- Each Erasmus Mundus programme has its own application portal and specific deadline within the broader January–February window — start researching specific programmes at least 6 months in advance.
- Apply to multiple EMJM programmes if your interests allow, since the scholarship pool and competition level varies by programme and field.
- Strong English (or relevant teaching language) proficiency scores are essential — sit your language test well ahead of application deadlines to avoid delays.

**⏰ Deadlines vary by individual programme but generally fall between January and February (Annual) — check each programme's specific page for its exact closing date.**
