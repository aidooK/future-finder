## About This Opportunity

The Swedish Institute Scholarships for Global Professionals (SISGP) is a fully funded Master's scholarship aimed at developing future leaders who will play a key role in addressing global challenges. The scholarship targets professionals from a defined list of countries, including Ghana, who have demonstrated leadership experience and a commitment to creating positive change. SISGP scholars join an active alumni network and benefit from a structured Leadership Development Programme alongside their academic studies.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Citizen of one of SISGP's eligible countries (Ghana is included — confirm current country list at si.se)
- Hold a Bachelor's degree and meet the specific entry requirements of your chosen Swedish Master's programme
- At least 3,000 hours (around two years) of relevant work or organisational experience
- Demonstrated leadership experience, such as leading teams, projects, or community initiatives
- Admitted to a Master's programme at a Swedish higher education institution before applying for the scholarship

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full tuition fees for your Swedish Master's programme
- Monthly living stipend
- Travel grant to and from Sweden
- Insurance coverage during your studies
- Membership in the Swedish Institute's Network for Future Global Leaders, including a structured leadership development track

## How to Apply

1. First secure admission to an eligible Master's programme at a Swedish university through [universityadmissions.se](https://www.universityadmissions.se)
2. Visit [si.se](https://si.se/en/apply/scholarships/swedish-institute-scholarships-for-global-professionals/) to confirm your country's eligibility and review the scholarship's specific criteria
3. Prepare your CV, leadership essay, and motivation letter as required by the scholarship application
4. Submit your scholarship application through the Swedish Institute's online portal
5. Scholarship decisions are typically released a few months after the application deadline, ahead of the academic year start

## Tips

- You must apply to and be admitted into a Swedish Master's programme first — the scholarship application is a separate, subsequent step, so plan your timeline carefully across both processes.
- SISGP places heavy weight on demonstrated leadership. Use concrete examples — leading a team, initiative, or community project — rather than general statements about leadership potential.
- Sweden's university application deadline (often in January) comes before the SISGP scholarship deadline (February) — make sure you don't miss the earlier university admissions window.

**⏰ Deadline: Mid-February (Annual) — but note the Swedish university admissions deadline comes first, typically in mid-January. Start your university application early.**
