## About This Opportunity

The Holland Scholarship is a Dutch government and university-funded programme offering a one-time grant of €5,000 to outstanding non-EEA students, including applicants from Ghana, who are beginning a Bachelor's or Master's degree at a participating Dutch research university or university of applied sciences. While not a fully funded scholarship, it provides meaningful financial support toward tuition and living costs in your first year of study in the Netherlands.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Non-EEA nationality (Ghanaians and most African nationals are eligible)
- Have not previously obtained a Dutch Bachelor's or Master's degree, or currently be enrolled in a Dutch higher education programme
- Be admitted, or in the process of applying, to a full-time Bachelor's or Master's programme at a participating Dutch institution
- Meet your chosen institution's specific academic and language entry requirements

## What You Get / Benefits

- A one-time grant of €5,000 applied toward your first year of tuition or living costs
- Access to study at one of the Netherlands' well-regarded research universities or universities of applied sciences
- Exposure to the Netherlands' strong international student community and English-taught programme options

## How to Apply

1. Choose a participating Dutch university or university of applied sciences offering the Holland Scholarship — not all institutions participate, so confirm this first
2. Apply for, or secure, admission to your chosen Bachelor's or Master's programme
3. Visit [studyinholland.nl](https://www.studyinholland.nl/scholarships/holland-scholarship) to access the application form specific to your chosen institution
4. Submit your scholarship application directly through your university's international office, following their specific process and internal deadline
5. Scholarship decisions are typically communicated a few months after the application window closes

## Tips

- Not every Dutch university offers the Holland Scholarship — check the participating institutions list before committing to a specific programme application.
- Because this is a partial scholarship (€5,000), budget realistically for the remaining cost of your studies and living expenses in the Netherlands before applying.
- Apply as early as possible within the February–May window, since some participating universities allocate scholarships on a rolling or first-come basis within the overall deadline.

**⏰ Deadlines vary by institution, generally falling between February and May (Annual) — confirm your specific university's internal deadline at studyinholland.nl.**
