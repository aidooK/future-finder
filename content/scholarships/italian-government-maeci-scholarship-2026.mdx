## About This Opportunity

The Italian Government MAECI Scholarship, offered by the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation, supports international students — including applicants from Ghana and other African countries — who wish to pursue Master's or PhD studies at Italian universities and academies. The scholarship is part of Italy's broader effort to strengthen academic and cultural ties with countries around the world through its diplomatic network.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Open to international applicants from countries with active bilateral cultural cooperation with Italy, including most African nations
- Hold a Bachelor's degree (for Master's applicants) or a Master's degree (for PhD applicants) relevant to your chosen field
- Apply through the Italian Embassy in your home country, as applications are processed via diplomatic channels (in Ghana, this is the Italian Embassy in Accra)
- Meet the specific language requirements of your chosen Italian programme — either Italian or English depending on the course

## What You Get / Benefits

- An annual grant of €9,000 to support your studies and living costs in Italy
- Access to Italy's historic and highly regarded university system, including programmes in design, engineering, the arts, and sciences
- Cultural immersion and academic exposure to one of Europe's major academic hubs

## How to Apply

1. Visit [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it) to confirm scholarship availability for Ghana and review eligible fields of study
2. Identify your target Italian university and Master's or PhD programme
3. Contact the Italian Embassy in Accra to confirm the local application process and any additional documentation required
4. Prepare your application, including transcripts, language certification, and a clear statement of study purpose
5. Submit your application through the official MAECI online portal before the annual deadline (typically late March)

## Tips

- Applications are processed through the Italian Embassy in your home country — start by contacting the Italian Embassy in Accra early to understand Ghana-specific requirements and any local interview steps.
- If your target programme is taught in Italian, factor in language preparation time well ahead of your application — Italian language proficiency is often a hard requirement for non-English-taught courses.
- The grant amount (€9,000/year) is solid but may not cover all costs in Italy's larger cities — research cost of living in your target city to plan your budget realistically.

**⏰ Deadline: March 26 (Annual) — confirm Ghana-specific requirements with the Italian Embassy in Accra well in advance.**
