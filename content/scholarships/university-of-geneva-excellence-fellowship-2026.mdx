## About This Opportunity

The University of Geneva Excellence Master Fellowship supports outstanding international students to pursue a Master's degree at one of Europe's most internationally connected universities, located in the heart of Geneva — home to the UN, WHO, WTO, and hundreds of international organisations. The fellowship is awarded to the most academically exceptional incoming Master's students across all faculties.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Applying for a full-time Master's programme at the University of Geneva
- Outstanding academic record, typically in the top 10% of your Bachelor's graduating class
- Open to all nationalities — Ghanaian and other African applicants are eligible
- Must apply for the fellowship and submit required documents before the March 15 annual deadline
- Some fellowships are restricted to specific faculties or programmes — confirm eligibility for your target programme

## What You Get / Benefits

- Annual fellowship of CHF 10,000 to CHF 15,000 (amount varies by faculty and award level)
- Study in Geneva, giving unparalleled proximity to international organisations and the global diplomatic community
- Access to the University of Geneva's strong programmes in international relations, law, life sciences, economics, and humanities

## How to Apply

1. Visit [unige.ch](https://www.unige.ch/en/a-propos/presentation/distinctions/bourses-excellence/) to review fellowship details and eligible programmes
2. Apply for admission to your chosen Master's programme at the University of Geneva
3. Submit a separate fellowship application before March 15, including academic transcripts and references
4. Shortlisted candidates may be contacted for additional information or an interview

## Tips

- Geneva's unique position as a global diplomatic hub makes this fellowship particularly valuable if you are interested in international law, public health, diplomacy, international relations, or humanitarian affairs — proximity to the UN and WHO creates remarkable professional networking opportunities.
- The fellowship amount is solid but Geneva is among the most expensive cities in Europe — plan your budget carefully and consider supplementary part-time work where permitted.
- Programmes at the University of Geneva are offered in both French and English depending on faculty — confirm your target programme's teaching language before applying.

**⏰ Deadline: March 15 (Annual) — confirm your programme's eligibility and submit both your Master's application and fellowship application before this date.**
