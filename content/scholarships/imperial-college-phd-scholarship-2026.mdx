## About This Opportunity

Imperial College London is one of the world's leading STEM-focused universities and offers a range of fully funded PhD scholarships to outstanding domestic and international students across its departments. Scholarships are primarily department-specific — meaning funding is allocated by individual research groups rather than through a single central award — making Imperial one of the most accessible UK universities for international PhD funding if you target the right department and supervisor.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Applying for a full-time PhD programme at Imperial College London
- Strong academic record, particularly at Master's level, in a STEM, business, or relevant interdisciplinary field
- International students including Ghanaians are eligible — many departmental scholarships explicitly include funding for overseas-fee-paying students
- Identify and secure interest from a prospective Imperial supervisor before applying — this is typically essential for funded PhD consideration

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full tuition fees (including overseas fees) for the duration of your PhD at Imperial
- Annual living stipend at a rate competitive with London's cost of living
- Access to Imperial's world-class laboratories, research centres, and industry partnerships
- Strong graduate job placement record, particularly in engineering, life sciences, and technology

## How to Apply

1. Visit [imperial.ac.uk/study/pg/fees-and-funding/scholarships](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/study/pg/fees-and-funding/scholarships/) to browse department-specific scholarship listings
2. Identify a research group and supervisor whose work aligns with your proposed PhD topic
3. Contact the supervisor directly with a brief research proposal before submitting your formal application
4. Apply to the PhD programme through Imperial's graduate admissions portal, indicating your interest in funding
5. Confirm department-specific scholarship deadlines — these vary significantly and some have early closing dates

## Tips

- Imperial's PhD funding is highly decentralised — your success depends heavily on identifying the right supervisor whose research group has available funding. Cold outreach to potential supervisors with a targeted research proposal is not optional; it is the primary pathway to funded places.
- Imperial is globally top-ranked in engineering, medicine, life sciences, and business — if your research sits within these areas, the competition is fierce but the opportunities are substantial.
- London's cost of living is high — when evaluating a funding offer, confirm that the stipend is calibrated to London expenses, as this varies between research groups and funding sources.

**⏰ Deadlines vary by department — check imperial.ac.uk for your specific department's scholarship listings and contact potential supervisors as early as possible, ideally 6–12 months before your target start date.**
