## About This Opportunity

The EDUFI Fellowship, administered by the Finnish National Agency for Education (EDUFI), supports doctoral students and postdoctoral researchers from outside the EU/EEA to conduct research stays of 3 to 12 months at Finnish higher education institutions or research institutes. The fellowship is designed to promote international academic mobility and strengthen Finland's research collaborations globally, including with African institutions and researchers.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Doctoral student or holder of a doctoral degree from a country outside the EU/EEA (Ghana qualifies)
- Must have a confirmed host supervisor or research group at a Finnish university or research institution
- Research proposal must clearly align with the work of your Finnish host
- Applications submitted through EDUFI's online portal with supporting documentation from both applicant and host

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend of €1,500 for doctoral candidates or €2,100 for postdoctoral researchers
- The fellowship covers stays of 3 to 12 months in Finland
- Access to Finland's cutting-edge research institutions, particularly strong in technology, life sciences, education, and design

## How to Apply

1. Identify a Finnish university or research institute and secure a supervisor or research group willing to host you
2. Visit [oph.fi/en](https://www.oph.fi/en/statistics-and-publications/edufi-fellowship) to review the current fellowship guidelines and application form
3. Prepare a detailed research plan, CV, and a formal invitation letter from your Finnish host supervisor
4. Submit your application through EDUFI's online system before the February 1 deadline
5. Results are typically communicated several months after the deadline

## Tips

- You must have a confirmed Finnish host before applying — this is a hard requirement, not a suggestion. Start contacting potential Finnish supervisors at least 6 months before the application deadline.
- Finland is a global leader in education research, clean technology, and digital innovation — these are particularly strong areas to pursue if they align with your doctoral work.
- The fellowship covers 3–12 months, so plan your research stay duration carefully to maximise what you can accomplish and what you need to demonstrate in your research plan.

**⏰ Deadline: February 1 (Annual) — confirm the exact date on oph.fi and begin reaching out to Finnish host supervisors at least 6 months in advance.**
