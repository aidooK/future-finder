## About This Opportunity

The Swiss Government Excellence Scholarship (SGES) is offered annually by the Swiss Confederation to support outstanding international researchers pursuing PhD studies, postdoctoral research, or short-term research stays at Swiss universities and institutions. The scholarship is part of Switzerland's commitment to international academic cooperation and is open to applicants from a wide range of countries, including several across Africa.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Hold a relevant Master's degree (for PhD applicants) or a PhD (for postdoctoral applicants)
- Be nominated by, or apply through, your country's Swiss embassy or designated national agency — applications generally go through your home country's official channel, not directly to Switzerland
- Strong academic record and a clear, well-defined research proposal aligned with a Swiss host institution
- Identify and secure interest from a potential Swiss supervisor or host institution before applying

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend ranging from CHF 1,920 to CHF 3,500 depending on your academic level (PhD vs postdoc)
- Tuition fee exemption or reduction at your host Swiss institution
- Contribution toward health insurance costs during your stay
- Access to Switzerland's world-class research infrastructure and academic networks

## How to Apply

1. Identify a Swiss university or research institution and a potential supervisor whose work aligns with your research interests
2. Contact your country's Swiss embassy or the relevant national nominating body to confirm Ghana's application process for SGES
3. Develop a clear, focused research proposal in consultation with your prospective Swiss host
4. Submit your application through the official national channel before the deadline (typically between September and December, depending on your country)
5. Successful candidates are notified several months later, ahead of the academic year

## Tips

- Securing a Swiss host supervisor's agreement before applying is essential — most successful SGES applications already have a confirmed institutional connection in Switzerland.
- Applications must go through your home country's official nomination channel, so contact the Swiss embassy in Accra early to understand Ghana's specific process and internal deadline.
- A focused, well-defined research proposal — rather than a broad area of interest — significantly improves your chances, since reviewers assess feasibility alongside academic merit.
