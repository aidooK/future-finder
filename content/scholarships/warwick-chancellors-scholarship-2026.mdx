## About This Opportunity

The University of Warwick's Chancellor's International Scholarship is a competitive fully funded award for outstanding international PhD students across any discipline at Warwick. It is one of the most generous PhD scholarships at a UK Russell Group university specifically targeting international students, covering both tuition fees and a living stipend for the full doctoral period.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Classified as an international (overseas fee-paying) student at Warwick
- Applying for a full-time PhD programme at the University of Warwick
- Ghanaian and African applicants are eligible — the scholarship specifically targets international students
- Strong academic record and a compelling research proposal

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full tuition fees for the duration of your PhD at Warwick
- Annual living stipend at a competitive UK doctoral rate
- Access to Warwick's research facilities, graduate school support, and academic networks

## How to Apply

1. Apply for your chosen PhD programme at the University of Warwick through the standard graduate admissions portal
2. Complete a separate Chancellor's International Scholarship application — this is distinct from your PhD application and must be submitted within the scholarship window
3. Prepare a strong research proposal and identify a Warwick supervisor willing to support your application
4. Submit both your PhD application and the scholarship application before the January deadline
5. Scholarship decisions are communicated alongside or shortly after admissions decisions

## Tips

- The separate scholarship application is a firm requirement — missing it means you will not be considered for the award even if you are admitted to the PhD programme.
- Warwick is particularly strong in economics, mathematics, computer science, social sciences, and business — applicants in these areas are competing against a particularly deep international pool.
- Contact your prospective Warwick supervisor well before the deadline — a supervisor who knows your work and actively supports your application is a significant advantage in the selection process.

**⏰ Deadline: Typically January (Annual) — check warwick.ac.uk for the exact current date and submit both your PhD application and the scholarship application simultaneously.**
