## About the Scholarship

The **Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program** is one of the largest and most prestigious scholarship programs for African youth. It partners with leading universities across Africa, North America, and beyond to provide fully funded education to talented young Africans who demonstrate academic excellence and commitment to giving back to their communities.

## What Is Covered

This is a **fully funded scholarship**. It covers:

- Full tuition fees for the duration of your program
- Monthly living stipend
- Round-trip airfare to your university
- Health insurance
- Academic support and mentorship
- Leadership development programming
- Alumni network access post-graduation

## Eligibility Requirements

- African citizen (all 54 countries)
- Demonstrated academic excellence (strong secondary school results)
- Financial need — from economically disadvantaged backgrounds
- Demonstrated leadership and commitment to community development
- For undergraduate: applying to a partner university
- For postgraduate: bachelor's degree holder with strong academic record

## Partner Universities (Sample)

- University of Ghana, Legon
- Ashesi University (Ghana)
- University of Toronto (Canada)
- Edinburgh University (UK)
- Sciences Po (France)
- And 30+ others worldwide

## How to Apply — Step by Step

1. **Identify your target partner university** — visit the Mastercard Foundation website and find the full list of partner institutions
2. **Apply to the university first** — you must gain admission before applying for the scholarship
3. **Complete the scholarship application** — available on the university's financial aid portal
4. **Submit your personal statement** — focus on your background, leadership experience, and commitment to Africa's development
5. **Provide two reference letters** — from teachers, mentors or community leaders
6. **Submit financial need documentation** — proof of household income

## Tips for a Strong Application

- Be specific and personal in your essay — selection panels read thousands of generic responses
- Quantify your community impact — "I tutored 15 students" is stronger than "I helped my community"
- Apply to multiple partner universities to maximize your chances
- Submit early — portals sometimes have technical issues close to deadlines
