## About This Opportunity

Estonia offers government-supported scholarships to outstanding international students and researchers pursuing Master's, doctoral, or postdoctoral study at Estonian universities. Estonia is internationally recognised for its advanced digital infrastructure and e-government innovation, making it an attractive destination for students interested in technology, digital governance, cybersecurity, and related fields. The country punches well above its size in academic quality relative to its cost of living.

## Eligibility Requirements

- International students outside the EU/EEA are eligible (Ghanaians qualify — confirm specific programme eligibility on studyinestonia.ee)
- Hold a relevant Bachelor's degree (for Master's applicants) or Master's degree (for PhD applicants)
- Meet the academic and language entry requirements of your chosen Estonian programme
- Scholarship eligibility is often tied to specific programmes or institutions — confirm directly with your target Estonian university

## What You Get / Benefits

- Monthly stipend of approximately €350 for Master's students and €660 for PhD students (amounts may vary by programme and year)
- Access to one of Europe's most digitally innovative academic environments
- English-friendly campus life, with many programmes taught fully in English

## How to Apply

1. Visit [studyinestonia.ee](https://www.studyinestonia.ee) to explore universities, scholarship options, and eligibility requirements
2. Identify your target Estonian university and programme and check its specific scholarship availability
3. Apply for admission through your chosen university's international admissions process — scholarship consideration is often integrated
4. Confirm application deadlines directly with your target institution, as they vary

## Tips

- Estonia is a global leader in e-government and digital innovation — if your field intersects with technology, digital policy, cybersecurity, or governance, this is an exceptionally relevant destination.
- Most Estonian Master's programmes are taught in English, which is a significant practical advantage for Ghanaian applicants compared to many other European scholarship destinations.
- Estonia is one of the smallest countries in Europe but has produced a disproportionate number of tech startups and unicorns — studying here connects you to a uniquely entrepreneurial academic culture.
