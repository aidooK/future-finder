## About This Opportunity

Denmark offers government-supported scholarships through its universities to outstanding non-EU/EEA international students pursuing Master's or PhD degrees. Unlike centralised national scholarship schemes, Danish scholarships are primarily administered by individual universities, which award tuition waivers and stipends from government-allocated funds to the most competitive international applicants. Deadlines and available awards vary by institution.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Non-EU/EEA nationality (Ghanaians and most African nationals qualify for non-EU scholarship consideration)
- Hold a relevant Bachelor's or Master's degree with strong academic standing
- Apply for admission to a Danish university through the university's standard international admissions process — scholarship consideration is typically integrated into the admissions review
- Meet the specific academic and English language requirements of your target programme (most Danish Master's programmes are taught in English)

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full or partial tuition fee waiver, depending on the institution and award level
- Some universities also offer a monthly stipend alongside the tuition waiver for highly competitive applicants
- Access to Denmark's innovative, English-friendly academic environment with strong links to industry and research

## How to Apply

1. Visit [studyindenmark.dk](https://studyindenmark.dk) to browse participating universities and available scholarship information
2. Identify a specific Danish university and Master's or PhD programme that interests you
3. Apply directly to the university's international admissions office — scholarship consideration is usually integrated into the application
4. Indicate your interest in scholarship funding in your application where prompted
5. Confirm programme-specific and institution-specific deadlines, as these vary widely across Danish universities

## Tips

- There is no single national application portal — you apply directly to each Danish university, so start with the institution you most want to attend and work from there.
- Denmark consistently ranks among the world's most innovative and sustainability-focused countries — strong candidates with backgrounds in green technology, design, engineering, or life sciences are particularly well-positioned.
- Most Danish Master's programmes are fully taught in English, making language access less of a barrier for Ghanaian applicants than in many other European countries.

**⏰ Deadlines vary by university — check your specific institution's website directly and apply early, as scholarship slots are limited and often awarded first-come among strong applicants.**
