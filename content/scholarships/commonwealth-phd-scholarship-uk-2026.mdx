## About This Opportunity

The Commonwealth PhD Scholarship, administered by the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission, fully funds doctoral study in the UK for talented students from developing Commonwealth countries, including Ghana. The scholarship is designed for researchers whose doctoral work will contribute meaningfully to development outcomes in their home country, supporting the UK's broader commitment to skills and capacity building across the Commonwealth.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Citizen of, or have been granted refugee status by, an eligible Commonwealth country (Ghana qualifies)
- Hold a first degree of at least upper second class (2:1) honours, and typically a relevant Master's degree
- Unable to afford to study in the UK without this scholarship
- Proposed research must demonstrate clear development relevance to your home country
- Apply through the nominating body in your home country

## What You Get / Benefits

- Full tuition fees for the duration of your PhD
- Monthly living stipend throughout your studies
- Return airfare to and from the UK
- Additional grants for thesis-related travel and research costs where applicable

## How to Apply

1. Visit [cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk](https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/scholarships/commonwealth-phd-scholarships/) to confirm eligibility and review supported research areas
2. Identify a suitable UK university and supervisor whose research interests align with your proposed PhD topic
3. Contact Ghana's nominating agency to confirm the national application process and internal deadline
4. Prepare a strong research proposal demonstrating development relevance, alongside transcripts and references
5. Submit through your nominating agency before their internal deadline, ahead of the Commission's final deadline

## Tips

- Securing a UK supervisor's interest before applying significantly strengthens your application — reach out to potential supervisors early with a brief research proposal.
- Your research proposal should explicitly connect your doctoral work to a development challenge facing Ghana or the wider region — generic academic proposals are less competitive.
- PhD scholarship competition is intense globally. Start preparing your full application package at least 6 months ahead of the deadline.

**⏰ Deadline: Typically late October (Annual) — confirm the exact date at cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk and check Ghana's nominating agency's internal deadline, which is often earlier.**
