The defending world champions begin their title defense tonight against an Algeria side that has quietly built one of the most balanced squads at this tournament. ## What to Watch For

Argentina will be without the same level of pressure they carried into the last tournament — they have already proven themselves. That can work two ways: relaxed champions playing with freedom, or a side that takes an early step too lightly against a disciplined Algeria team.

Algeria's best path to a result is keeping the game compact, frustrating Argentina's rhythm in midfield, and looking to hit on the counter through pace on the wings. A draw here would be considered a strong result for the Desert Foxes.

## Studying and Working in Argentina — What to Know

Argentina is not yet a mainstream study destination for African students compared to Europe, but opportunities do exist for those willing to look.

Argentina's public universities, including the University of Buenos Aires, are tuition-free even for many international students and rank among the best in Latin America. Spanish language proficiency is essential, and most African applicants pursue this route through bilateral cultural agreements between their home country's government and Argentina, or through direct university application.

For postgraduate research, Argentina's Ministry of Education and several national universities occasionally open scholarship calls for international students, though these are far less publicized than European or North American equivalents. If this interests you, the best approach is contacting the Argentine embassy in your country directly to ask about current bilateral education agreements.
