## Switzerland vs Bosnia and Herzegovina — Group B Clash

**Kickoff:** 3:00 PM ET | **Venue:** SoFi Stadium, Inglewood | **Group B**

Switzerland have built a reputation as one of the most tactically organized sides at every major tournament they enter, rarely beaten without a fight. Bosnia and Herzegovina bring genuine attacking talent and have shown they can trouble bigger nations when their forwards are firing.

This fixture should be tightly contested, with both sides needing a result to stay in control of their group.

## What to Watch For

Switzerland's defensive structure makes them difficult to break down, and Bosnia will need their wide players to create overloads and isolate defenders in one-on-one situations. Watch for Switzerland's set-piece routines — they have proven a reliable source of goals in recent tournaments.

A draw would not be a surprising outcome, but either side capable of snatching a late winner given the attacking quality on display.

## Studying in Switzerland — A World-Class but Different Pathway

Switzerland is home to some of the highest-ranked universities in the world, including ETH Zurich and the University of Geneva, and offers genuine opportunities for African students with strong academic records.

**Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships** are offered annually to international postgraduate students and researchers, including applicants from many African countries, covering both living costs and research support.

Switzerland is also a major hub for **international organizations** — the UN, WHO, and numerous NGOs are headquartered in Geneva, creating internship and early-career opportunities for African graduates interested in international development and diplomacy.

Tuition at Swiss public universities is also notably lower than many people assume, even for international students, making it worth researching beyond the country's reputation for being expensive.
