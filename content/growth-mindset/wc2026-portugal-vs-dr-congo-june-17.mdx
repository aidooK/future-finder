## Portugal vs DR Congo — David Meets Goliath

**Kickoff:** 1:00 PM ET | **Venue:** Houston Stadium | **Group K**

DR Congo's qualification for this World Cup is one of the great underdog stories of the tournament, and now they face one of the heaviest favorites in their group. Portugal arrive with elite talent across the pitch and the experience of multiple major tournaments behind them.

For Congo, simply being on this stage is already history. But football has a habit of producing surprises, and a disciplined defensive performance paired with one moment of magic could turn this into a story nobody forgets.

## What to Watch For

Portugal will likely dominate possession from the opening whistle, and Congo's success depends entirely on their ability to stay organized defensively for long stretches without conceding an early goal that opens the game up. Watch for Congo's wide players to look for quick transitions the moment they win the ball back.

A heavy defeat would not be surprising given the gap in overall squad depth, but Congo have shown throughout qualification that they thrive when written off.

## Studying in Portugal — Pathways for African Students

Portugal has deep historical ties with Lusophone Africa, and its education system reflects that connection.

**The Camões Institute** runs scholarship programs specifically aimed at students from Portuguese-speaking African countries — Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, and São Tomé and Príncipe — covering both undergraduate and postgraduate study.

Beyond the Lusophone-specific pathways, Portuguese universities increasingly participate in **Erasmus+ partnerships**, which open doors for African students from non-Portuguese-speaking countries as well, particularly at the postgraduate level.

Portugal has also become an attractive option for African tech talent more broadly, with several universities and startup hubs in Lisbon and Porto actively recruiting international students into technology and engineering programs.
