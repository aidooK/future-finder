## About This Opportunity

IBM, in partnership with BeMyApp, has launched the **AI Builders Challenge with IBM Bob** — a global program for university students to build real-world AI solutions using IBM technologies. Running **July through August 2026**, the Challenge features monthly innovation challenges: Creative Industries (July), Space Exploration (August), and a Wildcard Challenge on Intelligent Systems for the Future of Work.

This is a genuine chance to develop practical, in-demand AI skills — not just theory — while building a portfolio project that shows employers you're ready for an AI-driven economy.

## Eligibility Requirements

- University students, 18 years of age or older
- Enrolled at any institution of higher education (university, college, community college, technical or vocational school)
- Open to students from all majors — not just computer science
- Team size: 1–5 participants, solo participation also allowed

## What You Get

- Compete for a share of the **$15,000 USD total prize pool**, including a $5,000 Grand Prize for the top project across the Challenge
- Hands-on learning through IBM SkillsBuild and the IBM Bob Virtual Conference
- Access to mentors, office hours, and webinars during the Challenge period
- Recognition from IBM for top teams, plus the opportunity to showcase your work
- A public GitHub portfolio project you can show future employers

## How to Apply

1. Visit [aibuilderschallenge-bob.bemyapp.com](https://aibuilderschallenge-bob.bemyapp.com) to register
2. Complete IBM SkillsBuild learning content and the guided baseline notebook
3. Build your project using at least one IBM AI-supported technology (e.g. Granite, LangFlow, Docling)
4. Submit a public GitHub repository with a functioning prototype through the challenge platform

## Tips

1. **You don't need a computer science degree** — this is open to all majors. If you're curious about AI, this is a low-risk way to build real skills.
2. **Team up if you can** — teams of up to 5 are allowed, and combining skills (design, data, writing) often produces stronger submissions than solo work.
3. **Use it for your CV immediately** — a public GitHub project with a working AI prototype is a strong, concrete talking point in job and scholarship applications.
