## England vs Croatia — A Group L Heavyweight Clash

**Kickoff:** 4:00 PM ET | **Venue:** AT&T Stadium, Arlington, Texas | **Group L**

Croatia have made a habit of punching above their weight at major tournaments, reaching the World Cup final in 2018 and the semi-finals in 2022. England arrive with one of the most talented squads in the competition but a history of underperforming relative to that talent at major tournaments.

This fixture carries extra weight given Ghana and Panama are the other two sides in Group L — a result here could shape the entire group's complexion heading into the final round of matches.

## What to Watch For

Croatia's midfield experience and tactical discipline have repeatedly troubled bigger nations, and England will need to control tempo rather than get drawn into a physical, transitional battle. Watch how England's manager sets up against a Croatia side that thrives on patient, possession-based football of their own.

Set pieces and individual quality in the final third are likely to decide a tightly contested match between two sides who know exactly what is at stake.

## Studying in the UK — What African Students Should Know

The United Kingdom remains one of the most sought-after study destinations for African students, and two scholarship programs stand above the rest.

**The Chevening Scholarship** is the UK government's flagship international scholarship program, fully funding one-year Master's degrees for future leaders, influencers, and decision-makers from around the world, including across Africa. It is highly competitive but genuinely life-changing for recipients.

**Commonwealth Scholarships** are available to students from Commonwealth countries, which includes Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, and most of Anglophone Africa. These cover Master's and PhD study in the UK and are specifically designed to support development priorities in recipient countries.

Beyond these flagship programs, many UK universities also offer their own need-based and merit-based scholarships directly to African applicants — always worth checking on a university's own funding page when researching specific programs.
