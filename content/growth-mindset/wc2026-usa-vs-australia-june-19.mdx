## Studying in the USA — What African Students Need to Know

The United States hosts more international students than any other country in the world, and a range of scholarship programs specifically serve African applicants.

**The Fulbright Foreign Student Program** is the US government's most prestigious international scholarship, funding graduate study and research in the United States for outstanding students from around the world, including across Africa. Applications go through the US Embassy in your country.

**MasterCard Foundation Scholars Program** partners with several leading US universities to fund African students through undergraduate and graduate study, combining financial support with mentorship and leadership development.

Many US universities also offer their own institutional scholarships to international students, and athletic scholarships remain a genuine pathway for African student-athletes with competitive sporting records.

The US visa process requires early preparation — the F-1 student visa has specific financial documentation requirements that African students often underestimate the timeline for.
